Find Your Idea

Weeks 1-2

Every business starts with an idea. But not just any idea — one that matches your skills, interests, and market demand. Don't overthink this phase. Pick something and start.

List 5 business ideas based on what you already know or enjoy. Don't judge — just write.

What do you need to start? Tools, materials, licenses. Most businesses start under {{ currency_symbol }}1,000.

check_circle

4. Pick One

Choose the idea with the best mix of demand, low cost, and personal interest. Commit for 90 days.

2

Set Up Your Foundation

Weeks 3-4

Before you make money, you need the basics: a way to get paid, a place to operate, and a system to track everything. Set these up once and they'll serve you forever.

account_balance

1. Business Banking

Open a separate bank account for your business. Keep personal and business money separate from day one.

receipt

2. Payment Method

Set up Stripe, PayPal, Square, or bank transfers. Make it easy for customers to pay you.

description

3. Simple Contract

Create a one-page agreement template. Covers scope, price, timeline, and payment terms.

sync_alt

4. Daily Finance Check

Start tracking opening balance, deposits, withdrawals, and closing balance every day.

tips_and_updates

finanTrace helps: Use the Reconciliation tool to check your daily numbers. It instantly tells you if anything is off and explains why.

3

Get Your First Customers

Weeks 5-8

This is the hardest part for most people. The secret? Start with people you already know. Then ask for referrals. One customer leads to the next.

people

1. Tell Everyone

Tell friends, family, and social media followers what you're doing. First customers often come from your network.

campaign

2. Find First 3

Identify 3 potential customers. Offer a discount or free trial. Focus on getting testimonials, not profit.

star

3. Get Reviews

Ask every happy customer for a review on Google, Yelp, or social media. Social proof drives new business.

repeat

4. Referral System

Create a simple referral program. "Refer a friend and you both get 10% off." Your best customers bring more.

tips_and_updates

Pro tip: Most businesses fail because they stop marketing after the first sale. Always be looking for the next customer. Follow your profession path for daily checklists.

4

Track & Optimize

Months 2-3

Once you have customers, you need to know if you're actually making money. Track everything, find what's working, and do more of that. Cut what isn't working.

monitoring

1. Track Revenue

Record every dollar that comes in. Know your monthly revenue, best-selling products, and peak seasons.

receipt_long

2. Track Expenses

Every dollar out matters. Categorize expenses (materials, marketing, tools). Know your profit, not just revenue.

compare_arrows

3. Find What Works

Which marketing channel brings the most customers? Which product has the best margin? Double down on winners.

trending_up

4. Set Growth Goals

Set a monthly revenue target. Break it down: "I need 5 new customers at {{ currency_symbol }}100 each." Track progress weekly.

tips_and_updates

finanTrace helps: The Finance Hub tracks income vs expenses and shows your accuracy rate. See exactly how your business is performing.

Scale & Systemize

You've proven your business works. Now it's time to grow without burning out. Build systems, hire help, and expand. This is where real wealth is built.

repeat

1. Automate

Automate invoicing, email responses, scheduling, and bookkeeping. Spend your time on high-value work.

groups

2. Hire Help

Hire a virtual assistant or freelancer for tasks you shouldn't be doing. Focus on growth, not busywork.

add_circle

3. Expand Offerings

Add new products, services, or packages based on what your customers already buy from you.

map

4. Plan Next Phase

Use the Business Roadmap to plan your next 6-12 months. Set milestones and track progress.
